About Me

Marine Biologist & Science Communicator

I'm passionate about understanding and protecting our ocean ecosystems. From a long-term academic foundation and hands-on field experience, I want to combine hard research with creative storytelling to progress science and its accessibility.

My work has focused on water contamination, habitat conservation and restoration, and documenting biodiversity through research and photography. I believe that effective conservation requires both solid science and the ability to connect people emotionally with the natural world.

When I'm not in the field or lab, you'll find me diving and hiking, photographing loved ones and things, reading (a lot about nature of course), and enjoying the time I have with friends and family. An often ignored contingence of science and research is universal benefit from it; science is for well-being. Enjoying its benefits - in my field, things like clean beaches, safe water, interconnected spaces - are good for the well-being of everything in that environment, including humans. Including us.

Research & Projects

LA Water Contamination Study

Student Research Assistant

Under the Moffett Lab, run by James Moffett, I work with Mia Bradshaw, a graduate student at the University of Southern California. We collect fresh and seawater samples from rivers and bays in the Los Angeles area to test for metals, specifically the rare earth metal Gadolinium as a marker for septic leaks and for excess nutrient runoff, as well as locate it as it is a contaminant.

Field Research Data Analysis Analytical Chemistry

Habitat Biodiversity and Productivity Assessment

Student Researcher

Comprehensive survey between 0-20 meters where benthic cover, small vertebrate, and large vertebrate biodiversity were observed and quantified, contributing to long-term monitoring data of Galapagos marine ecosystem health. Observations advise adaptive and novel conservation and restoration strategies, especially for pelagic species. The project included monitoring cover, survival rates, growth patterns, and ecosystem recovery over 4 months to provide hard research to combine with community observations through public apps and organizations.

Landscape Justice Initiative

Student Researcher and Representative

Under the University of Southern California, campus projects have joined with landscaping management to initiate soil tests for replanting native, climate-adapted plants around campus. This project allows undergraduate research to be integrated into coursework, graduate research for background information and means of integration, and staff to literally implement the project on campus, including the environmental and economic benefits around saving water and increasing natural, unmaintained productivity.

Volunteer Work

I have had opportunities globally to volunteer through anthropogenic and ecological avenues. My last major volunteering opportunity was informing and acting on restorative farm practices in mainland Ecuador and the Galapagos Archipelago, as well as trail maintenance to sensitive ecosystems on uninhabited islands used for tourism. I have partaken in Community Science initiatives including Shark Count and collecting public data for macroinvertebrates in the Galapagos Islands and at Shoals Island in Maine as well as volunteer with beach cleanups with organizations like Surfrider in Maine and California.
